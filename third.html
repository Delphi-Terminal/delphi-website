AI-driven stocks have significantly outperformed broader markets over the past year

By Sahana Lydia

Date

AI-linked equities are not just outperforming but are driving the market. Over the oast 12 months companies like AMD (+88%), Google (+75%), and Nvidia (+53%) have outdone significant markets including, the S&P 500 at just 19%.

The reason is a capital shift. Investors are aggressively relocating into companies that benefit from AI. Specifically those building computer infrastructure, supplying chips and monetizing AI through software and data.

AI now is becoming the market’s primary growth driver. Not contingent on its current earnings but its future dominance. The forward expectations replicate the structure shift similar to internet and cloud computing, where early leaders are expected to get long-term value. Resulting in major capital flow to pioneers in the market.

This also explains the divergence. Semiconductors and infrastructure names leading as the first layer of the AI buildout with software and data companies to follow. The rest of the market not directly tied to this cycle, being left behind.

The significance of this is market structure. When these levels of inflow are caused by a small group of stocks, the entire index becomes dependent on them.  

When leadership is this narrow, the turning point doesn’t come the market broadly but from the leaders themselves. The moment those names stop outperforming everything adjusts. Index gains stall, volatility rises and capital rotated out of leaders into lagging sectors or out of equities entirely.

As long as AI drives returns, the market stability is maintained. But if they don’t the markets underlying fragility becomes evident quickly.
