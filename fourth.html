Political pressure is rising for the Federal Reserve to cut rates but markets are pricing the opposite.

By Sahana Lydia

Date

President Donald Trump told reporters on Monday that the U.S. Federal Reserve should hold a “special meeting” to cut interest rates “right now”.  

The pressure is straightforward as growth risks are rising and the Federal Reserve should act preemptively.

However, markets are not buying it.

According to Delphi Market, prediction markets are assigning near certainty around 99% that the Fed will hold rates steady, with no probability of a cut.

The arguments for cuts is to prevent an economic slowdown before it happens. However, the markets have repriced it differently.

Here is why:

  • inflation is above target
  • Labor market has not materially weakened
  • Although financial conditions have tightened they aren’t restrictive enough to force action

Using monetary policy in this condition would risk reversing progress on inflation.

This is not a market waiting for the Fed to act. it is a market recognizing that the Fed does not have a reason to. Until that changes cuts remain a narrative not a reality
