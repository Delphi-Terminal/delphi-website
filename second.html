Top market mover: Crude oil

Oil markets have surged over the past month, driven by geopolitical ridk centered on the Strait of Hormuz.

By Sahana Lydia

March 17, 2026

Crude oil has climbed more than 50% in recent weeks with WTI trading near $95.40 per barrel and Brent above $102.90. This change hasn’t been driven by demand but by a geopolitical repricing.

The center of the repricing is the Strait of Hormuz, where a fifth of the world’s oil supply passes. Recent tensions in the region have forced markets to price in the possibility of major disruption. Not caused by supply being cut but because of the cost of being wrong is too high.

The move being globally driven is reinforced by Brent’s premium over WTI widening. A result of Brent being more sensitive to international supply routes like Hormuz, pricing a higher risk of disruption than the domestically anchored WTI.

Oil markets move not on what has happened but on what could happen. If the tensions were to escalate further or shipping through Hormuz is threatened, current oil prices aren’t the peak but merely an early stage of repricing.

This is a market waiting for an event. When that event arrives, oil will not adjust gradually but move all at once
