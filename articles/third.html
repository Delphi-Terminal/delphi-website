AI Driven Equities Significantly Outperformed Broad Markets Over the Past 12 Months

AMD, Google, Palantir, and Nvidia led gains while broad indices and sectors lagged.

By Sahana Lydia Mar 17, 2026

AI linked equities are not just outperforming, they are driving the market. Over the past 12 months, companies like AMD (+88%), Google (+87%), Palantir (+75%), and Nvidia (+53%) have far outpaced broad indices: the S&P 500 at 19%, the Nasdaq 100 at 25%. The rest of the market, Healthcare and Financials at 5% and 2%, has been left behind.

The reason is a capital shift. Investors are aggressively relocating into companies that benefit from AI: those building computer infrastructure, supplying chips, and monetizing AI through software and data.

AI is becoming the market's primary growth driver. Valuations reflect not current earnings but future dominance, a structural shift similar to the internet and cloud computing eras, where early leaders captured long term value. The result is major capital flow into the pioneers of this cycle.

That explains the divergence. Semiconductors and infrastructure names lead as the first layer of the AI buildout; software and data companies follow. The rest of the market, not directly tied to this cycle, lags.

The significance is market structure. When this level of inflow is concentrated in a small group of stocks, the entire index becomes dependent on them.

When leadership is this narrow, the turning point doesn't come from the market broadly but from the leaders themselves. The moment those names stop outperforming everything adjusts. Index gains stall, volatility rises and capital rotates out of leaders into lagging sectors or out of equities entirely.

As long as AI drives returns, market stability holds. If it does not, the underlying fragility becomes evident quickly.
