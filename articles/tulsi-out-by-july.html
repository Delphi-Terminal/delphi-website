Iran Intelligence Dispute Puts Tulsi Gabbard Under Pressure

Difference in intelligence? The difference between views of national intelligence I mean.

By Viviana Chen Mar 18, 2026

Director of National Intelligence and 2020 presidential candidate Tulsi Gabbard has history for criticising Trump as he speeds toward war with Iran and has publicly crossed him (claiming there was no evidence Iran was building a nuclear weapon).

On March 17, 2026 , head of National Center for Counterterrorism Intelligence official Joe Kent resigned saying “Iran poses no imminent threat,” placing the spotlight on Tulsi Gabbard and how she’ll acts during the Senate Intel Hearing. Her past views aligning with Kent’s explain why the crowd believes she will resign a month earlier. But recently, she defended Trump’s military action against Iran, saying it’s the president’s responsibility to determine what is an imminent threat or not.

Knowing the risk her position is at, she likely re-evaluates her stance in accordance and states Trump has reviewed intelligence and has acted appropriately as commander in chief. This has already caused the resurfacing of her anti-war positioning from 2020 and hints closer at her resignation.

Are these predictions switching like her stance in the war? Not necessarily, but definitely could.

What This Means

Before Jul 1 line has crossed with Before Aug 1. Obviously indicative of friction for her position. Lines are expected to cross further at any and every misstep during the hearing, putting internal intelligence officials at risk of re-electing a new director. This destabilization of US internal affairs in the face of war will cause investors to reassess geopolitical risk, shift to safe-haven assets, and price in for a longer, more inflationary Iran conflict for US stocks.

However, in another market on Who will leave the Trump administration this year, Delphi quotes a 24.5% (midprice) chance Gabbard leaves and 21.8% adjusted for buy and sell pressure. This suggests traders are skeptical that her position is at risk and are waiting to see what she’ll say at the Senator Intel Hearing.
