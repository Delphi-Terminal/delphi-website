President Donald Trump told reporters on Monday that the Federal Reserve should hold a special meeting to cut interest rates right now. The logic is simple: growth risks are rising, and the Fed should act before a slowdown takes hold.

[[IMAGE]]

Markets are not buying it. According to Delphi Market, prediction markets assign near certainty, around 95%, that the Fed will hold rates steady. The probability of a cut is effectively zero.

The case for cuts is to get ahead of an economic slowdown. Markets have priced it differently. Three reasons:

- Inflation remains above target
- The labor market has not materially weakened
- Financial conditions have tightened but are not restrictive enough to force a move

Cutting in this environment would risk undoing progress on inflation. The Fed has no clear reason to act.

This is not a market waiting for the Fed. It is a market that has already decided the Fed will not move. Until the data shifts, rate cuts stay in the realm of narrative, not policy.
