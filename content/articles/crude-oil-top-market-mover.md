Crude oil has climbed more than 50% in recent weeks with WTI trading near $95.40 per barrel and Brent above $102.90, showing the market is pricing geopolitical risk and not stronger demand.

[[IMAGE]]

The center of the repricing is the Strait of Hormuz, where a fifth of the world's oil supply passes. Recent tensions in the region have forced markets to price in the possibility of major disruption. Traders are paying up because the downside of being wrong is severe.

The move being globally driven is reinforced by Brent's premium over WTI widening. This is a result of Brent being more sensitive to international supply routes like Hormuz, pricing a higher risk of disruption than the domestically anchored WTI.

## What This Means

Oil markets move not on what has happened but on what could happen. If the tensions were to escalate further or shipping through Hormuz is threatened, current oil prices are not the peak but merely an early stage of repricing.

The crude oil market is waiting for just one event to possibly change everything, and that very quickly.
