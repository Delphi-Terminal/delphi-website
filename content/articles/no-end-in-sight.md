CNN says there’s “No End in Sight” but markets show it couldn’t be further from the truth.



CNN’s “no end in sight” segment has acted as a deliberate narrative repeated across at least three separate pieces between March 16th and March 23. 



On March 16, CNN’s analysis declared “there’s no deal in sight” and “no sign of a diplomatic off-ramp”. On March 20, Christiane Amanpour anchored her segment with an open-ended escalation “with no end in sight”. On March 23, CNN used a Stephen Collinson analysis quoting Senator Chris Murphy: “This war is spinning out of control. Prices are spiking for millions of Americans… There’s no end in sight.” 



However, prediction markets disagree. Loudly. 



“No End in Sight”: The Ceasefire Timeline



According to Delphi Markets, the largest unified API for prediction market data, contracts have processed over $31.7 million in ceasefire bets, $4.4 million in end-of-operations contracts and $741k in recession wagers since the conflict began on February 28. The consensus across all of them is clear, this will end by the summer, the economy will absorb the hit and nothing is spiraling anywhere. 



And here is what happens when you hold the “no end in sight” narrative up against that volume.



First and foremost, Polymarket’s US-Iran ceasefire market is one of the platforms’s most actively traded contracts, with $31.7 million in total volume and 9 potential resolution dates. As of March 23, 2026 this is what the probability curve looks like:



Ceasefire by April 15: 26%

Ceasefire by April 30: 40%

Ceasefire by May 31: 51%

Ceasefire by June 30: 56%

Ceasefire by December 31: 72%





Separately, the $4.4 million market volume contract, “Trump announces end of military operations against Iran by..” gives a 73% probability by June 30. That’s in three months. Doesn’t look like there’s “no end in sight.”



And on whether the “US will declare war on Iran by December 31?” markets sit at just 9%. Traders overwhelmingly signal this will not be a prolonged war, with even the most bearish contracts implying a resolution well within 2026. 



There is an end in sight according to prediction markets. Polymarket is accurate more than 94% of the time a full month before an outcome is definitively known. An accuracy rate built when real money in on the line.



Prices Are Elevated, Not “Spiraling Out”



CNN’s framing of markets “spiraling” implies that current market prices represent unprecedented, out of control chaos but historical data shows a completely different story. Every major asset class has seen either higher or comparable levels within the last four years alone.



Oil (Brent Crude)

Current price: $113 per barrel as of March 23, 2026.



In March 2022, when Russia invaded Ukraine, Brent crude surged above $120 per barrel after the EU imposed sanctions on Russian oil, peaking at around $128. The all time record for $147 per barrel in 2008. Today’s $113 is 11% below the 2022 Ukraine war peak and 23% below the all-time high. Though it is definitely elevated it isn’t close to being unprecedented. The oil market over the past 15 years has absorbed shocks of this magnitude and recovered both times. 



Markets have already begun correcting. Brent spiked to $119 on March 19, then dropped $6.31 within 24 hours to $107.40 on March 20 before settling around $113. Prediction markets agree – “Crude Oil hitting an all time high by March 31” dropped from 24% to 4% in just 14 days after opening on March 9.



Gold

Current Price: $4,267 per ounce as of March 23, 2026.



Gold hit its all time high of $5,595 on January 29,2026, well before the Iran war even started. Since then, it has corrected downward significantly. This week saw gold’s worst weekly performance in years, dropping close to 9%. If prices were truly “spiraling out”, gold of the ultimate safe-haven asset should be surging to new highs. Instead, the “panic” narrative doesn’t survive contact with traders also betting a 93% probability gold “will be under $4500 by the end of March.”



S&P 500

Current level: ~6507 as of March 20, 2026 down 5.1% year to date.



After Trump’s “Liberation Day” tariff announcements in April 2025, the S&P 500 dropped by approximately 19% until recovering in full over the summer. In 2022, the index fell 25% during the bear market while the COVID crash saw a 34% decline in a single month. Even the VIX, the market’s fear gauge has spiked to 23-27 – elevated, but nowhere close to the 40+ readings that characterize a real market crisis.



Bitcoin

Current price: $70,335 as of March 23, 2026.



Bitcoin reached its peak at $126,198 in October 2025. The current 44% decline from that peak is steep but well within the normal of cycle corrections. In 2022 Bitcoin fell from $69,000 to $16,000, a 77% collapse. The current pullback is roughly half the severity of the last cycle’s correction and still 4x higher than its 2022 bear market lows.



The Economy isn’t Spiraling Either

Beyond the ceasefire timing, prediction markets depict whether the broader economy is “spiraling”. And on every single metric. The answer is no.



“US recession by end of 2026?” contract carries $741k in volume and prices in only a 37% chance of recession. 63% of the volume highlights that the economy avoids a downturn entirely. The Fed’s own upgraded GDP forecast for 2026 is a 2.3% growth, not a contraction.



The Fed also held rates steady at its March meeting, with a 95% market-implied probability of no change at the April meeting either. The central bank isn’t in crisis mode and neither is the money



The Only Thing Spiraling Is The Narrative.



CNN’s coverage of the Iran war serves a purpose: driving viewership and urgency. However, it is just not an accurate depiction of what the world’s largest pool of real-money predictors believes. When synthesizing the data a clear consensus emerges. A 56% chance of ceasefire by June. A 73% chance Trump announces end of operations by July. Oil below its 2022 peak. Gold falling, not surging. Stocks down 5%, not 25%. No recession.



CNN chose "no end in sight" three times in seven days. Tens of millions of dollars in prediction market volume chose the opposite.
